Class Syllabus

This is the class website for the Professor Burke's Chem 137 lecture (computational chemistry) for spring quarter 2011. You will find the syllabus below, as well as useful links in the menu on the left.

Office hours

4-5pm Wednesday and Friday in NSII 2145.

Lucas Wagner

Classroom

  • Lecture meets in RH192, MWF from 9-9:50am.
  • Lab meets in the modeling facility on the second floor of NSII. There are two sections of lab on Monday, one at 4pm and one at 5:30pm. The lab will also be available to you from 4-6pm on Wednesday and Friday. Find Prof Burke, Nate, or Lucas (NSII 1202) to unlock the door if necessary.

Textbook

Frank Jensen, Introduction to Computational Chemistry, 2nd ed. (John Wiley & Sons, Hoboken, NJ, 2007).

Grading

  • 20% midterm
  • 40% HW, incl lab assignments
  • 40% project
  • 30% final - optional, and cannot reduce grade.

Midterm

Friday, April 29 (end of Week 5)

Project

Based on some work in the literature, you will do some DFT or MD simulations and write your own mini-paper. You submit the paper and give a 10 minute talk in last week of class.

Timeline:

  • End of week 4 (Fri, Apr 22, 9am):

    Submit a pdf of a paper involving either a DFT or MD simulation about something you find interesting. Think of a question related to the paper. Suggest a title and brief plan of your project.

  • Start of 8th week (Mon, May 16, 9am):

    Submit draft of your 'paper'.

    Requirements of paper:
    brief abstract, at least one figure and one table, at least 5 references from literature introduction, body, conclusions. No more than 1500 words text. Everything precisely formatted.

  • 10th week:

    10 minute presentations on projects, no labs.
